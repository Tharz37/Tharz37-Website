PID Self Balancing Bot using Aries V3 Board

The Balancer Bot and the "Black Box" Board

Every robotics student has seen a PID balancing bot. It’s a classic project, almost a rite of passage. You take a sensor, a motor, and a microcontroller, and you use a PID algorithm to make it stand. We thought we knew exactly what we were getting into. Our plan, however, had a twist: we weren't using a standard Arduino or Raspberry Pi. We chose to use a brand new, indigenous ARIES board.

This decision immediately turned a straightforward project into a deep, complex challenge. When a board is new, it means you are in uncharted territory. There are no Google search results, no forum posts, and most importantly, no software libraries. Our biggest problem was the MPU-6050 gyroscope... Our bot was effectively blind, and our project was at a dead end.

Custom Library for IMU

This is the kind of problem that stops a project cold. But this is also where my teammate, Shahazad Abdulla, truly shined. Instead of giving up and switching to an easier board, he took on the challenge of taming this new hardware.

He didn't just find a library; he created one. He dug into the datasheets and protocols, engineering a completely custom MPU-6050 library from scratch, specifically for the ARIES board. It was a massive breakthrough, and a significant contribution to the board's new community.

View the Custom Library on GitHub

Victory: More Than Just a Balancer

With Shahazad’s code, our "black box" was finally open. We could get clean data from the gyro, feed it into our PID algorithm, and tune the system. Watching the bot flicker, fight gravity, and finally stand stable on its own was more than just a project success; it was a victory. We didn't just build a balancer; we built the tools to make it possible, proving that our team can solve problems right down to the hardware level.
