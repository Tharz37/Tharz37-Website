Light Follower: My First Engineering Project

My First Year, My First Line of Code

This project is a memory. It was my first year, and our team (Shahazad, Sidharth, Nakul, and I) had a mix of backgrounds. Sidharth and Nakul were from computer science, but Shahazad and I were from bio-maths. To make it more intimidating, I knew absolutely nothing about programming—I was a complete rookie. Shahazad, on the other hand, was already a master with previous project experience. I just assumed he would do all the coding and I would help.

That's not what happened. He gave me the Arduino and all the sensors, saying "Don't worry, you can do it. If any error occurs, ask GPT." He didn't know I didn't even know how to make a code work. But my ego wouldn't let me give up.

The Build: How It Works

I was determined to build it myself. I sat down and figured out the logic, purely hard-coded with minimal AI help. The concept is simple: compare the light from three LDRs to steer the bot. Here are the components and connections I used.

Components Required
-------------------
- Arduino Uno
- L298N Motor Driver
- 3 LDR Sensors (Light Dependent Resistors)
- 2 BO Motors
- Wheels & Chassis
- 12V Battery / Power Supply
- Jumper Wires

Circuit Connections
-------------------
Component     Arduino Pin
LDR Left      A0
LDR Center    A1
LDR Right     A2
Motor ENA     9
Motor IN1     8
Motor IN2     7
Motor ENB     10
Motor IN3     6
Motor IN4     5

Working Principle
-----------------
1. The LDR sensors detect light intensity.
2. The Arduino compares LDR values to find the brightest direction.
3. Motors are controlled via the L298N driver:
   - Move Forward: Center LDR gets the most light.
   - Turn Left: Left LDR gets more light.
   - Turn Right: Right LDR gets more light.
   - Stop: No significant light is detected.

The Lesson: Just Try

When I finally uploaded the code and the robot's wheels actually turned to follow my flashlight, I was so happy and proud. It was more than just a working project.

It was proof that even if you don't know anything about a subject, if you are determined to try, you can make it work. This first project, and the push from my teammates, is what started my whole journey.
