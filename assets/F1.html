90s Era Model Car (Inspired)

A geometric baseline study inspired by late-90s F1 architecture. The primary objective of this phase was to establish a valid mechanical packaging layout (wheelbase, track width, and driver positioning) before advancing to complex aerodynamic surfacing. This model served as the control geometry for initial CFD setup in Ansys Fluent, allowing for rapid meshing and boundary condition validation without the computational overhead of high-curvature surfaces. It demonstrates my understanding of fundamental vehicle constraints and systematic model preparation.

2022 inspire front wing

An advanced surface modelling study exploring the 2022 "Ground Effect" aerodynamic regulations. This project prioritized high-quality surface continuity (G2/Curvature) to minimize flow separation and drag. Key features include a multi-element front wing designed to manage front-wheel wake (outwash generation) and a tight-wrapped engine cover created using complex lofting and boundary surface techniques in Fusion 360. The design intent was to balance downforce generation with clean airflow delivery to the rear diffuser, validated through surface curvature analysis.
